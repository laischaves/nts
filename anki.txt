How do we calculate *springs in series*?; we consider the load on each spring to be the same, $k_1y=F$ (1) and $k_2(x-y)=F$, therefore, it works like parallel resistors would: $$k_{eq}=\frac{1}{\frac{1}{k_1}+\frac{1}{k_2}}$$

How do we calculate *springs parallel to each other*; the equivalent stiffness constant will be = $$k_{eq}=(k_1+k_2)$$

How do we calculate *dampers parallel to each other*; the equivalent stiffness constant will be = $$b_{eq}=(b_1+b_2)$$

How do we calculate *dampers in series*, we consider the load on each spring to be the same, $b_1\dot{y}=F$ (1) and $b_2(\dot{x}-\dot{y})=F$, therefore, it works like parallel resistors would: $$b_{eq}=\frac{1}{\frac{1}{b_1}+\frac{1}{b_2}}$$
